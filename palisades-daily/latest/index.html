Executive Summary

Today is moderately busy, with the most practical items around rebuilding risk, civic input, and local recovery logistics.

The clearest homeowner takeaway: contractor fraud enforcement is now active in the Palisades Fire zone, so residents should check license status and documentation before engaging rebuild help.

The main civic deadline is July 16, when the city is scheduled to present updated plans for the Potrero Canyon bridge connecting George Wolfberg Park to Will Rogers State Beach.

There are also community-life updates: Palisades Fourth of July events are returning, local groups received support for a BBQ and celebration, and Pali High’s Maggie Gilbert Aquatic Center remains open for public lap swim by reservation.

Key Stories

  • Unlicensed contractor charges in the Palisades Fire zone: NBC Los Angeles reports six people were charged after state investigators reviewed ads without contractor license numbers and arranged undercover bid appointments in Pacific Palisades. Practical takeaway: check CSLB license records before hiring anyone for demolition, grading, ADUs, or rebuild work.
  • Palisades renters allege failed remediation and face eviction: The Palisadian-Post reports tenants at 15757 West Sunset Boulevard claim unresolved smoke, habitability, and maintenance issues after the fire while the landlord demands back rent. The landlord and counsel did not respond to the Post’s requests for comment.
  • Fourth of July support and return of local traditions: Palisades News reports the Community Council approved two $5,000 grants: one for a free Lowe Family YMCA community BBQ at Simon Meadow and one for the Palisades Americanism Parade Association’s July 4 events.
  • Pali High pool access: The Maggie Gilbert Aquatic Center page says public lap swim is open by reservation, with adult, senior, and student pricing listed. It also notes closed-campus entry rules and restrictions on private lessons or team training without a permit agreement.

Decisions Affecting Palisades

  • Potrero Canyon bridge meeting set for July 16: The Bureau of Engineering plans a virtual meeting from 5:30 to 7 p.m. on the proposed bridge from George Wolfberg Park to the Will Rogers State Beach parking lot. Westside Current reports construction is not yet authorized and the city still has alternatives, environmental review, and cost questions ahead.
  • Council District 11 also flags the bridge, road work, and repairs: CD11 says residents and stakeholders are encouraged to reengage on the bridge proposal. The same update says asphalt repairs have begun around the Palisades and Bienveneda Avenue emergency repairs are ongoing, with traffic impacts expected Monday through Friday, 7 a.m. to 4 p.m.
  • LADWP water system update at PPCC: LADWP told the Community Council that the Santa Ynez Reservoir cover replacement is expected to be complete and refilled by December, with backup measures in place. LADWP also said it is replacing 100,000 linear feet of pipe over six years and has installed 22 new fire hydrants along the route.

Fire/Rebuild Watch

  • Interactive rebuild map: The Palisadian-Post’s Pali Rebuild Map lets residents look up permit status, construction progress, and estimated completion dates by address. This is useful for homeowners, builders, Realtors, and neighbors trying to understand block-by-block progress.
  • Rebuild dashboard: The Post’s dashboard, using PaliBuilds data current as of May 31, tracks plan-submittal-to-permit timing, permit-to-certificate-of-occupancy timing, and LADWP power-meter installation timing going forward.
  • County recovery resources: LA County’s Department of Economic Opportunity page lists recovery resources for businesses and workers, including One-Stop Permit Center information and links to broader fire recovery support.
  • Insurance and rebuild estimating advice: A Palisadian-Post contributor piece from Monument Home Builders argues homeowners should get their own construction-cost support when evaluating insurance estimates. Treat it as vendor advice, not an official source.

Around The Web

  • What Remains archive: A Palisades teen created a digital archive for photos and stories of objects that survived the fire or were accidentally carried out during evacuation. Palisadians can contribute through the project site.
  • Bioremediation workshop near Will Rogers Beach: California Community Foundation highlights Resilient Palisades’ workshop on using fungal mycelium and other ecological methods to help address post-fire soil contamination and runoff concerns.
  • Palisades Towne Hall concept: The Palisadian-Post previously detailed a movable, modular marketplace planned for Via de la Paz with short-term leases and crowdfunding support. It remains worth watching as a commercial-recovery experiment.

Watch List
