Executive Summary

Today’s local picture is fairly quiet, with more community-recovery signals than major new government actions.

The most practical item is a tax-timing reminder: some fire-affected homeowners may still be able to elect prior-year federal casualty-loss treatment, but the Palisadian-Post article frames July 15 as the review date to avoid missing the October 15 election deadline.

On the everyday-local side, Pali Trading Post is now open on West Sunset, and the Fourth of July race and parade gave the town a visible post-fire community milestone.

Fire recovery remains active in the background: wildfire relief funding, infrastructure requests, contractor-fraud enforcement and rebuild-tracking tools are worth watching, but most are continuations rather than fresh action items.

Key Stories

  • Pali Trading Post opens on West Sunset

The new general store and gift shop opened July 1 at 15248 West Sunset Boulevard. It carries home goods, Palisades-themed gifts and apparel, with many vendors described as local or community-linked.

  • Will Rogers race returns with 1,583 registered runners

The race returned to its Pacific Palisades course Saturday morning, with 1,162 registered for the 5K and 421 for the 10K. For residents, the main takeaway is community continuity: the event happened, though organizers noted volunteer challenges because many people remain displaced.

  • Fourth of July parade returns

The Palisadian-Post reports the Fourth of July parade returned to Pacific Palisades less than 18 months after the fire. Treat this as a community-recovery marker rather than a new action item.

Decisions Affecting Palisades

  • NBC Los Angeles: LA to receive millions more from Newsom’s wildfire relief fund

NBC Los Angeles reports LA County is set to receive an additional $200 million from the governor’s wildfire relief fund. The report says about $142 million would help underground utility lines in the Palisades and Eaton burn areas, with additional money approved for Palisades fire hydrants and water meters.

  • Patch: Park urges Newsom to direct unspent disaster funds to Palisades recovery

Councilwoman Traci Park asked the state to extend the June 30 disaster-fund deadline and front money for Palisades infrastructure needs, including streets, curbs, sidewalks, slope stabilization, streetlights, signs, storm drains and mobile surveillance trailers.

Fire/Rebuild Watch

  • Palisadian-Post: The October Deadline Most Palisades Homeowners Don’t Know About

Fire-affected homeowners should ask a qualified tax professional whether the federal casualty-loss election applies to them. The article says the October 15, 2026, deadline may affect whether a 2025 disaster loss can be claimed on a 2024 return.

  • 121 Design Build: Pacific Palisades Rebuild Map owner’s guide

The rebuild map remains useful for neighborhood visibility, parcel status and public-record tracking. The practical caveat: it is not an official permitting authority, and owners still need to rely on LADBS, LA City Planning and project professionals for decisions.

  • FOX 11: 6 unlicensed contractors charged in wildfire recovery sting

The unlicensed-contractor case now has near-term court dates: FOX 11 reports three arraignments are scheduled for July 8, while others have an August 5 preliminary hearing. For homeowners, the practical reminder is still to verify licenses before signing rebuild work.

Around The Web

  • Circling The News: Letter on local firefighters saving homes

A resident letter gives a first-person account crediting local firefighters from Stations 23 and 69 with saving homes during the Palisades Fire. It is perspective and memory rather than a new official finding.

  • Heal the Bay: Reflections one year after the Palisades Fire

Heal the Bay’s president reflects on displacement, return and post-fire water-quality work. The useful takeaway is that the group says clearer state guidance is still needed for post-fire recreational-water testing and public-health benchmarks.

  • LinkedIn / L.A. Business First: Pali Trading Post mention

L.A. Business First also highlighted Pali Trading Post as a local general store aimed at restoring a small-town feel.

Watch List

  • State wildfire-relief money: watch whether Palisades-specific street, sidewalk, hillside, lighting, storm-drain and public-safety requests receive funded approvals beyond the county allocation described by NBC Los Angeles.
  • Contractor-fraud enforcement: the July 8 and August 5 court dates may produce more detail on how the county is policing rebuild-zone contracting.
  • Rebuild tracking: the private rebuild map may help residents compare public records with what they know about their own parcel, but discrepancies should be checked against official city systems.
  • Tax timing: homeowners with unreimbursed fire losses should not wait until October to ask whether the casualty-loss election matters for their 2024 or 2025 filing strategy.
