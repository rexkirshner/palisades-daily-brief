Key Stories

Palisades Village gets an Aug. 15 reopening date

Los Angeles Times and Westside Current report that Palisades Village will reopen to the public Aug. 15 after more than $100 million in fire-related work. Westside Current says the center is 99% leased, with most former tenants returning and nearly one-third of businesses new to the center.

Why it matters: this is a practical marker for the return of a major local gathering and shopping district.

PPCC candidate deadline is July 23

Palisades News lays out the rules for running for Pacific Palisades Community Council Area Representative or At-Large Representative. Candidates must submit a statement of no more than 200 words and a current photo by 5 p.m. July 23.

Area candidates must live in the area they seek to represent; At-Large candidates may qualify through residence, property ownership or business operation in Pacific Palisades. The article says special provisions apply for people affected by the January 2025 Palisades Fire.

Supervisor Horvath to speak at Palisades business breakfast

Palisades News reports LA County Supervisor Lindsey Horvath will speak at the Pacific Palisades Business Association’s Connections Breakfast on July 25 from 8 to 10 a.m. at Gladstones.

If you run a local business or want a county-level recovery update, this is the most concrete civic/business event newly surfaced today.

Fire/Rebuild Watch
