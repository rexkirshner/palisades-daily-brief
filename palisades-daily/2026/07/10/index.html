Key Stories

Rebuild map shows 27 completed new homes

The Palisadian-Post’s interactive Pali Rebuild Map reports, through July 9, 1,628 submitted new-home permits, 1,087 issued permits, 904 homes started, and 27 new homes with certificates of occupancy.

Practical takeaway: the map is useful for neighborhood-scale visibility, but it also notes that completion dates are estimates and have shifted after updated inspection logic.

Rebuild sequencing: architect, contractor and the rest of the team

The Palisadian-Post walks through why many fire-rebuild homeowners should think carefully about when to bring in an architect, contractor, engineers, surveyors and other specialists.

Practical takeaway: if you are early in the rebuild, this is a good skim before signing up for a one-stop package or waiting until plans are complete to talk construction cost.

Water reuse has to be planned before rough plumbing

Palisades News highlights a water-reuse company’s argument that homeowners need to separate greywater plumbing early if they want to irrigate with reused water later.

Practical takeaway: the useful point is timing. If water reuse matters to you, ask your architect, plumbing engineer or contractor before rough plumbing, not after walls are closed.

Palisades Village reopening details keep filling in

Beverly Press, Patch and NBC Los Angeles all report the Aug. 15 reopening after more than $100 million in renovations and remediation.

The most useful new detail: Palisades Village is described as 99% leased, with most former tenants returning and nearly one-third of businesses new to the property. Returning names include Loomey’s Toys, K Bakery, Elyse Walker and Angelini; new additions include Spacca Tutto and LESET.

Fire/Rebuild Watch

Unlicensed contractor charges remain a rebuild warning

Palisades News reports six people were charged with allegedly operating as unlicensed contractors in the Palisades wildfire disaster area. Prosecutors say bids collectively totaled as much as $1.27 million.

Practical takeaway: before hiring anyone, verify licensing and be cautious with disaster-area solicitations.

Arson case allegations are background, not an immediate resident action

AOL, carrying The Independent, reports on prosecutors’ pretrial allegations about the defendant’s state of mind in the Palisades Fire arson case. The defendant has pleaded not guilty, and the defense argues he is being scapegoated.

Practical takeaway: this is important context for the fire’s legal aftermath, but it does not change day-to-day rebuilding steps today.

Habitability lawsuit highlights standing-building recovery disputes

A Turner Law Firm post describes a lawsuit involving a Sunset Boulevard apartment building near Palisades Charter High School, alleging post-fire habitability, rent and eviction issues.

Practical takeaway: if you are a tenant or landlord dealing with smoke, ash, remediation or reoccupancy questions, this is a reminder to document conditions and get qualified advice.
