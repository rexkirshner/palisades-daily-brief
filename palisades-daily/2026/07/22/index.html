Key Stories

Riviera outage shows how disruptive small utility failures can be

A Palisadian-Post report says at least 52 households lost power Tuesday from around 4 a.m. until 10:15 p.m. LADWP crews worked near Amalfi and Romany drives after finding significant damage to an underground junction box.

Why it matters: the outage hit during a summer heat wave, and early restoration estimates slipped repeatedly.

Ciela recognized in senior-living ratings

Palisades News reports that Ciela Senior Living in Pacific Palisades was recognized in U.S. News & World Report’s 2026 ratings for assisted living, memory care and independent living.

Why it matters: not urgent, but useful if you track local elder-care options or the recovery of local services.

The Willows promotes its DK-8 program

Palisades News carried a video item describing The Willows Community School as a DK-8 school with 470 students from more than 51 ZIP codes.

Why it matters: this is more school-profile than news, but it is locally relevant for families comparing school options.

Decisions Affecting Palisades

Appian Way closure starts July 27

Palisades News reports that Appian Way is scheduled to close for one week starting July 27 so crews can remove part of the nearly 90-year-old Santa Monica Pier bridge.

Practical takeaway: this is outside the Palisades, but it may matter for beach, pier or coastal-route trips south of town.

Fire/Rebuild Watch

Insurance pressure remains a background issue

Straight Arrow News reports that Gov. Gavin Newsom has warned insurers, banks, credit unions and mortgage lenders about delays or denials affecting fire survivors.

Practical takeaway: read this if you are dealing with insurance or lender-controlled funds; otherwise it is process background.

Smoke-damage claims require documentation

Apex Adjusting Group’s guide argues that smoke contamination can be physical property damage and lists examples of documentation owners may need.

Practical takeaway: useful if you are still evaluating smoke, HVAC, contents or remediation claims; not a new official action.

Fire debris is still part of the beach-cleanup reality

The Palisadian-Post essay “Soup du Jour?” describes a Will Rogers Beach cleanup with Resilient Palisades members and notes that pieces of fire-damaged homes are still washing ashore.

Practical takeaway: not a policy update, but a grounded reminder that recovery is still showing up in public spaces.

Rebuild-hardening lessons continue to circulate

CSRwire summarizes why some homes survived the January 2025 fires, including fire-resistant construction, covered openings, defensible space and vegetation management.

Practical takeaway: skim if you are still making rebuild design choices; do not treat it as a Palisades-specific permit guide.
