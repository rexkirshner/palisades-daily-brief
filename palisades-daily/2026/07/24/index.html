Key Stories

Pali Rebuild Map explains its permit-counting method

The Palisadian-Post piece explains why the map counts one primary permit per parcel instead of every permit associated with a property. Practical takeaway: raw permit totals can overstate visible rebuild progress because a single home can require several permits.

LAPD says reported Palisades crime has subsided, but rebuild-site theft reporting matters

At PPCC, LAPD Senior Lead Officer Brian Espin said reported Part I crime fell from roughly eight a week earlier this summer to one last week, while residents described repeated thefts from construction sites. The useful action in the Palisadian-Post report: file reports, because unreported repeat thefts are hard for LAPD to track.

PCH pedestrian signal planned for Palisades Bowl crossing

The Palisades News item says Caltrans awarded a contract to replace the blinking pedestrian light at Palisades Bowl Mobile Home Park with a pedestrian-activated signal. Materials are expected in about seven weeks, with installation to follow.

Veronica Beard will return when Palisades Village reopens

Palisades News reports Veronica Beard will return to Palisades Village when the center reopens August 15. The article says it is unclear whether the brand will occupy its previous Swarthmore Avenue location.

Decisions Affecting Palisades

Marquez Knolls rebuild guidance emphasizes early view-impact review

The Palisadian-Post summarizes the Marquez Knolls View Impact Analysis process and warns that LADBS approval does not equal compliance with private CC&Rs. Practical takeaway for Marquez Knolls rebuilds: talk to neighbors and address view impacts before plans are finalized.

PPCC leadership and elections remain a civic participation thread

In a Palisadian-Post letter, Maryam Zar reflects on leaving the PPCC executive committee and encourages residents to consider running for area representative or at-large seats as elections approach. Treat this as civic background unless you are considering PPCC service.

Fire/Rebuild Watch

Recovery report says many survivors remain displaced or financially strained

The Daily News reports on a Department of Angels recovery survey covering Altadena and the Palisades. It cites financial strain, depleted savings, debt, delayed repairs or rebuilding, and continued displacement as major recovery gaps.

Architects describe rebuild decision fatigue and ways to reduce paralysis

A Palisadian-Post article frames rebuilding as a “second disaster” of insurance, housing, permitting, contractors, and hundreds of decisions. Practical takeaway: sequence decisions, mark milestones, and use professionals to reduce avoidable cognitive load.

Fire rebuild architect guidance is useful but vendor-sourced

A Gerami Architectural Corp. explainer says a licensed architect is not automatically required for every conventional single-family rebuild, but complex hillside, structural, grading, drainage, or code issues may require licensed professional involvement. Useful as general orientation, not an official rulebook.
