Key Stories

Rebuilt home ready, but full power still not on

ABC7 reports that Jack Nguyen’s family moved into a rebuilt Pacific Palisades home after receiving a certificate of occupancy, but still had only low-wattage construction power. The family said LADWP had been non-responsive since July 2.

Why it matters: this is a practical rebuild bottleneck. The report says multiple residents have described similar trouble getting power turned on after completing rebuilds, which could matter more as more homes finish.

City waives many rebuild permit and clearance fees

Palisisades News reports that Mayor Karen Bass signed an urgency ordinance waiving a wide range of city permitting and clearance fees for qualifying wildfire repairs and rebuilds.

The practical rule: the waiver applies up to 110% of the original structure’s floor area, is capped at $90 million over three years, and applies only to people or entities that owned the property on Jan. 7, 2025. LADBS is still working out refund procedures for eligible fees already paid.

K Bakery is coming back to Palisades Village

K Bakery plans to reopen Aug. 15 at 1061 Swarthmore Avenue in Palisades Village. Owner Kim Kedeshian lost her home and had to close K Bakery and Armav after the fire.

The new version will focus on a smaller menu, mostly to-go service with some patio seating, pastries, cakes, breads, coffee, tea, lemonade, breakfast and lunch items.

Palisades Village reopening details continue to firm up

FashionNetwork says Palisades Village is scheduled to reopen Aug. 15 after more than $100 million in environmental remediation and structural repairs. It reports the center will return almost fully leased.

A separate Palisadian-Post story says Blue Ribbon Sushi will return with an expanded patio and a new 14-seat omakase dining experience called Ueki Omakase.

Decisions Affecting Palisades

PPCC has a new president

Circling The News profiles Allison Holdorff Polhill, who was sworn in as Pacific Palisades Community Council president on July 1. The story emphasizes her LAUSD, school-board, PPCC, infrastructure and rebuild experience.

Practical takeaway: PPCC leadership matters because the council remains one of the main local civic interfaces with city and county officials during recovery.

Potrero Canyon bridge process draws mixed resident reaction

The Palisadian-Post reports that more than 130 people attended the July 16 online meeting on the proposed pedestrian/bicycle bridge connecting George Wolfberg Park to Will Rogers State Beach.

The project is still in predesign, approval and environmental documentation phases, with construction estimated to be complete in 2032. The cost estimate is $19 million to $26 million, with $11 million in state funding tied to the project and not transferable to other uses.

Fire/Rebuild Watch

Temescal Gateway Park reopening gets a human-scale follow-up

The Palisadian-Post followed 92-year-old Marjorie Friedlander back into Temescal Gateway Park on reopening morning. The useful access update remains: Sunset and Rivas trailheads are open, as are Sycamore Grove and Rubell picnic areas, while the waterfall trail remains closed.

The story also notes longer-term infrastructure work: MRCA authorized a land-use agreement allowing LADWP temporary use of part of the park during repair work.

Palisades Fire retrial schedule set

NBC Los Angeles reports that a judge set the next steps for Jonathan Rinderknecht’s federal arson retrial. The second trial is scheduled to begin Oct. 19, with an Aug. 19 hearing on jury selection and questionnaires and Sept. 30 argument on motions.

Treat this as background unless you are tracking the case closely.

Insurance pressure story is relevant but centered outside the Palisades

NBC Los Angeles reports on Eaton Fire families disputing whether smoke-damaged homes are safe to reoccupy. It also says 38% of Los Angeles fire survivors report they have already run out of, or soon will run out of, additional living expense coverage.

This is not a Palisades-specific case study, but the insurance issue is relevant to fire survivors across the Los Angeles fires.
