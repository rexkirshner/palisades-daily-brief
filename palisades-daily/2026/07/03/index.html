Top 3

  1. 1

    Extra security is planned in the Palisades for July 4 illegal-fireworks enforcement. LAPD, LAFD, park rangers, Rec and Parks and Councilwoman Traci Park’s office are coordinating added coverage; all fireworks remain illegal in Los Angeles.

    CD11’s latest Palisades update says SBA disaster-loan borrowers now have up to 24 months from authorization to access funds. The same update says rebuilding permit-fee waivers were approved, Phase 2 asphalt repairs have begun, and brush clearance remains required.

Executive Summary

Today is practical and local: holiday fire prevention, rebuild financing and road work are the items most likely to affect residents immediately.

For July 4, the key resident takeaway is simple: use the official parade and evening celebration, not fireworks. Officials say enforcement will be heavier around open spaces and hillsides.

For rebuilders, CD11 reports two useful updates: SBA disaster-loan access has been extended for approved borrowers, and city rebuilding permit-fee waivers have moved from suspension to official approval and refunds.

Key Stories

  • Free trauma recovery workshop for fire survivors: A free workshop is scheduled for 1 to 3 p.m. Saturday, July 11, at the Palisades Woman’s Club, 901 Haverford. The focus is trauma and grief symptoms, practical regulation skills, breakout groups and Q&A; registration is by email to Maria Gray.
  • Pali Trading Post ribbon cutting: CD11 highlights Pali Trading Post as a new local business and community gathering place opened by Michelle Murphy. Worth noting if you are tracking everyday commercial life returning, not just home construction.
  • July 4 celebration details: The Palisades Fourth of July Parade begins at 4 p.m.; an evening celebration at Palisades Charter High School runs from 6 to 9:30 p.m. with music, food trucks and a drone show.

Decisions Affecting Palisades

  • Rebuilding permit-fee waivers approved: CD11 says the city has officially approved rebuilding permit-fee waivers for Palisades rebuilding efforts, enabling refunds and implementation after fee collections had been suspended.
  • ULA relief for disaster victims remains pending: CD11 says relief for disaster victims delayed by a final procedural vote is expected to move forward after a vote in August. This matters for Palisadians who lost property and may need to sell.
  • Palisades Disaster Recovery District recommendations: The Palisades Recovery Coalition published recommendations from 25 randomly selected residents on what a potential disaster recovery district should fund and how it should remain accountable. This is still a shaping-the-process item, not a finished policy.
  • Statewide home-insurance surcharge ruling: A Los Angeles County Superior Court judge upheld wildfire surcharges on California home policies tied to the FAIR Plan bailout after the January 2025 fires. This is background for Palisades homeowners watching insurance costs, not a new local deadline.

Fire/Rebuild Watch

  • SBA disaster-loan access extended: Approved borrowers may have up to 24 months from loan authorization to access funds. CD11 says the extension is intended to help homeowners and businesses slowed by permitting delays and insurance disputes.
  • Brush clearance reminder: CD11 says all Pacific Palisades is in a high fire severity zone, and property owners within 200 feet of any structure, including incomplete rebuilds, must clear weeds, excess brush and dead vegetation under LAFD guidelines.
  • Two Palisades rebuild permits appear in a citywide construction-permit roundup: The Real Deal lists permits for an about 11,000-square-foot two-story home on North Rimmer Avenue and an about 8,000-square-foot two-story home on North Ocampo Drive.
  • Unlicensed contractor charges remain a caution for rebuilders: The District Attorney’s Office says six unlicensed contractors were charged after alleged bids totaling up to $1.27 million in the Palisades Fire disaster zone. If hiring, verify licensing before money changes hands.

Around The Web

  • “The Palisades Comeback Is Real”: A Palisadian-Post opinion piece by a homebuilder argues that permitting, active construction and lot sales show rebuilding momentum. Treat it as an argument from an interested industry voice, but the cited rebuild figures are useful context.
  • Pacific Palisades micro-neighborhood guide: A real-estate guide summarizes how Palisades areas differ by walkability, lot pattern, views and rebuild context. Useful for neighborhood orientation, not civic decision-making.
  • Private-school overview for 90272: Private School Review lists six private schools serving 1,461 students in 90272 for the 2026 school year, including Corpus Christi, St. Matthew’s, Calvary Christian, Seven Arrows and Village School.
