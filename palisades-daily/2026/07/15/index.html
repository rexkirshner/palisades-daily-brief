Key Stories

Temescal Gateway Park reopening is the most practical local update

Temescal Gateway Park will reopen Friday, July 17, at 8 a.m. The supplied park notice says this marks a major Palisades Fire recovery milestone. Treat it as a useful access change, but note that carried-forward local calendar material says the waterfall trail remains closed.

Loomey’s Toys plans a Palisades return

Loomey’s Toys plans to reopen in Palisades Village on Nov. 1 after its original Pacific Palisades location burned in the January 2025 fires. The store has been operating a second location in Laguna Beach, which is expected to remain open.

Resident takeaway: this is another concrete sign that family-oriented retail is coming back to the Village, beyond the previously covered Aug. 15 shopping-center reopening.

Public safety: hate-crime charges and a reported attempted break-in

JFeed reports that Bruce Lion faces three felony hate-crime charges after allegedly threatening Rabbi Zushe Cunin during a Shabbat prayer service at Cunin’s Pacific Palisades home. The story says Lion has been held on bail since arraignment and could face prison time if convicted.

Separately, Citizen reported an unconfirmed attempted break-in at 226 Giardino Way, with two men allegedly fleeing in a dark-colored SUV. Treat the Citizen item as a situational alert rather than a fully developed police report.

Palisades Village reopening remains the main retail milestone

A KTLA item carried by AOL says Palisades Village will officially reopen Aug. 15, with returning tenants including Loomey’s Toys, K Bakery, elysewalker, A.L.C., Erewhon and Zimmermann, plus new tenants including Spacca Tutto, LESET, Violet Grey and Xirena.

This is mostly confirmation of the same reopening milestone already covered recently, so the practical update today is to keep Aug. 15 on the calendar and watch for event details.

Decisions Affecting Palisades

Coastal Commission rebuild bill remains future-facing

A CalMatters story carried by The Signal says state lawmakers are considering a bill that would give the California Coastal Commission more oversight when new buyers rebuild homes destroyed by future disasters.

The practical caveat: the story says the bill would not apply to homes destroyed by last year’s fires. For current Palisades rebuilds, this is policy background, not an immediate permitting change.

Mar Vista committee item is mostly a CD11 coordination issue

The Mar Vista Community Council’s Public Health and Safety and Homelessness Committee is set to discuss a proposed CD11 coordination policy for cross-boundary homelessness and public-safety issues.

This is outside the Palisades, but relevant as a Westside/CD11 governance item. Skim unless you follow neighborhood-council coordination closely.

Fire/Rebuild Watch

Northfield construction fire follow-up

NBC Los Angeles reports that William Pratt tried to help before firefighters arrived at the Northfield Street construction fire. LAFD said heavy fire was reported just before 4 p.m., 34 firefighters responded, no injuries were reported, and the cause remains under investigation.

For residents rebuilding nearby, the actionable point is unchanged: this remains a reminder to watch construction-site fire risk and emergency access.

Debris-removal milestone appears in federal materials

U.S. Army Corps material describes an Aug. 26 milestone marking debris removal at the final opt-in private property in the Palisades fire recovery area. The current material is thin and date-forward, so treat it as a watch item rather than a full local update today.
