Key Stories

Santa Ynez Reservoir is dry again while DWP outlines firefighting backups

Circling the News reports the Santa Ynez Reservoir is drained again because of cover work. DWP told the outlet six water tankers are pre-deployed, with additional fleet resources available as needed.

Resident takeaway: this is worth reading if you live near the wildland edge or want to understand what water sources DWP says are available before another major fire.

Caltrans completes PCH protective wall repairs

Caltrans says it completed two protective wall projects on PCH: a 460-foot catchment wall south of Big Rock Drive and a 710-foot retaining wall below the Getty Villa. The Big Rock project cost about $17 million.

The practical consequence is improved slope, rockfall, and fire/storm resilience on a key Palisades route. Caltrans says Palisades Fire recovery operations are still expected to continue through the year.

Palisades Village restaurant lineup is firming up

Palisades News reports the Village reopening will bring back Alfred, Angelini, Blue Ribbon Sushi, Erewhon, McConnell’s, and Porta Via, plus new additions Spacca Tutto and K Bakery.

This is less about novelty than normalcy: the Village reopening is becoming a real dining and gathering-place restoration, not just a retail reopening. SMDP separately reports the Aug. 15 reopening plan and says more event details are still to come.

Business Block preservation analysis gives preservation advocates a path

The Palisadian-Post reports a structural analysis commissioned by Councilmember Traci Park’s office found roughly half of the historic Business Block ruins can be saved.

Resident takeaway: the old “Starbucks building” site is not simply a total-clearance question. Preservation discussions with the owner are ongoing, and the Historic-Cultural Monument status adds review requirements.

Decisions Affecting Palisades

Marquez Knolls view protections remain a live rebuild issue

The Palisadian-Post reports a Los Angeles Superior Court ruling allows a Marquez Knolls post-fire rebuild dispute over CC&R view protections to proceed.

Practical takeaway: if you own in Marquez Knolls, do not assume the view-protection rules are irrelevant. The article frames this as the first in a series on neighborhood-specific rebuild issues.

Business Block preservation now depends on owner talks and review process

The Business Block preservation story also belongs here because it affects what can happen at one of the Village’s most visible historic sites. The key point is that an independent analysis found preservation is at least partly feasible.

Fire/Rebuild Watch

Recovery coordination remains a political and practical fight

The Center Square reports that state and local governments have streamlined permitting, but residents and businesses still face insurance disputes, rising material costs, contractor constraints, and calls for a more coordinated recovery authority.

Treat this as background unless you are tracking policy advocacy. It explains why individual homeowners still feel stuck even as official permitting and construction numbers improve.

Rec Center construction and tennis court access

Circling the News reports tennis courts 7 and 8 have been redone and that all eight courts may be open before August 1. The same meeting covered Rec Center construction planning, with grading expected once permits are final.

The useful point: courts are expected to remain open during future Rec Center construction, and bocce courts are also promised to stay open.

Palisades permitting help is still easy to find, but some items are service pages

The LADWP Palisades Inspections and Permitting Support Center remains a practical reference: 16925 Marquez Avenue, weekdays 9 a.m. to 5 p.m., with city departments on site for rebuild support.

Other permitting-related items in today’s results were private service pages, useful mainly as market signals rather than civic news.
