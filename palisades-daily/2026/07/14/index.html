Key Stories

Brush clearance around Palisades Country Estates is now a homeowner-versus-parkland issue

Circling The News reports that Palisades Country Estates residents are asking State Parks about brush clearance and dead trees near homes bordered by Topanga State Park and Santa Ynez Canyon Park.

Practical takeaway: if your lot backs onto public land, this is worth reading for the permit and responsibility issues. The article says State Parks told residents they need a paid permit for limited clearance near habitable structures, while city-side dead-tree removal was handled after a tree fell onto cars.

Local businesses are still operating in exile while trying to keep Palisades ties

Luxe Homecare lost its Palisades office and now operates from Brentwood, while its owners say they want to return to the Palisades long-term. David Tishbi Jewelry is marking one year at a temporary Montana Avenue location after losing its Via de la Paz storefront.

Practical takeaway: these are not policy stories, but they are useful signals about the commercial rebuild: some longtime businesses are surviving by serving Palisades customers from nearby neighborhoods.

Xirena adds to the Palisades Village reopening picture

Palisades News reports that Los Angeles apparel brand Xirena will open its first standalone store at Palisades Village when the center reopens in mid-August.

The same item says other new tenants include Spacca Tutto, LETSET and Violet Grey, while several prior tenants are expected back. This is mainly a local-business milestone, not a new resident action.

June crime map highlights construction-site and home-entry concerns

Circling The News says the June Part 1 crime map includes four burglaries from construction sites, four incidents involving transients entering homes and two aggravated assaults.

Practical takeaway: if you have a rebuild site or an unoccupied home, this is a reminder to review security and reporting practices. The article lists Senior Lead Officer Brian Espin as the contact for concerns.
