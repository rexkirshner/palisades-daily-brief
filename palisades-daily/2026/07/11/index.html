Key Stories

Northfield Street construction fire

A Palisadian-Post report says more than 30 LAFD firefighters extinguished a heavy fire at a two-story home under construction in about 20 minutes, with no injuries reported. LAFD said the cause is undetermined and arson investigators were requested.

Circling The News adds neighborhood detail: the home had already been damaged in the Palisades Fire and was being rebuilt. Practical takeaway: rebuild sites remain vulnerable, and neighbors are watching closely for fire spread, insurance, and construction-site safety implications.

Temescal Gateway Park remains closed; DWP yard approved

Circling The News reports Temescal Gateway Park is still fenced off after the fire and that MRCA adopted a resolution allowing DWP to use part of the park as a maintenance yard while undergrounding utilities and restoring service.

For residents, the issue is not just process. It affects hiking access, YMCA-adjacent activity, truck movement, and how long a major local open-space gateway remains limited.

Pali Trims offers lot and parkway cleanup

A Palisades High sophomore and friends have started Pali Trims to help clear weeds, debris, parkways and sidewalks around construction sites and homes where owners are not currently living nearby.

This is small but practical: empty lots and overgrown parkways are a visible rebuild-era problem, and the article includes a Google Form for residents interested in the service.

Phase 2 asphalt repairs are underway

Palisades News reports Los Angeles Bureau of Street Services has begun Phase 2 of asphalt repairs in Pacific Palisades, with work expected over the coming weeks.

Use caution around work zones, large trucks, heavy equipment and road crews.

Decisions Affecting Palisades

Temescal and DWP staging

The MRCA/DWP decision is the main governance item today. The key resident questions are how long the use lasts, whether park access changes during the work, and how truck traffic interacts with nearby family and YMCA activity. Read Circling The News if this is your park or commute corridor.

Potrero bridge meeting coming up

The July 16 Potrero pedestrian bridge meeting is the next concrete access-and-safety item on the calendar. It is worth tracking if you care about connecting Potrero Canyon to Will Rogers Beach without relying on unsafe PCH crossings.

Road repairs

Phase 2 asphalt repairs are underway. The immediate consequence is caution around work zones; the longer-term issue is whether repairs keep pace with construction traffic and post-fire roadway wear.

Fire/Rebuild Watch

Power Up Palisades launches in August

Resilient Palisades’ Power Up Palisades is a group purchasing program for solar and battery storage tied to rebuild planning. The practical point: solar, battery and prewire decisions are cheaper and easier before walls close.

The article says a community webinar is planned for August 3 and installers are expected at Pali Veg Fest on August 9.

Land values and lowball offers

A Palisadian-Post real estate column argues Palisades post-fire land values have stabilized after earlier declines and frames current offers against rebuild risk, permitting, infrastructure, insurance and owner psychology.

Treat it as market context, not a decision rule. It is most useful if you are weighing whether to sell a lot, rebuild, or hold.

Smoke-damage insurance disputes

An AOL republication of a New York Post/ABC7-related story focuses on homeowners disputing whether smoke-damaged homes are safe to reoccupy, including a Pacific Palisades homeowner on Lachman Lane.

This remains background unless your own claim turns on habitability, remediation scope or temporary housing benefits.
