Key Stories

PCH safety push gets a documentary moment

The Palisadian-Post reports that local filmmaker Michel Shane’s documentary “21 Miles in Malibu” premieres July 9 on PBS SoCal. The practical takeaway: PCH safety remains a live regional issue for Palisadians who depend on the route, with Shane advocating for speed cameras, infrastructure fixes and modernization.

Santa Ynez Reservoir questions are back

Circling The News reports that Santa Ynez Reservoir is empty again and that an LAFD captain said at a June 10 event that local stations had only just learned of it. This is worth reading if firefighting water capacity and LADWP communication are on your rebuild-risk checklist.

PPCC candidate deadline is July 23

Palisades News notes that Pacific Palisades Community Council candidates must meet residency rules and submit 200-word statements by 5 p.m. July 23. If you want a stronger resident voice in recovery and infrastructure issues, this is the near-term civic deadline.

Since the reopening date already led a recent brief, the new value today is the tenant/event detail and calendar confirmation.

Decisions Affecting Palisades

Coastal Commission rebuild bill would affect future disasters, not this fire

A CalMatters story carried by The Signal says Senate Bill 1229 would require Coastal Commission approval for new buyers rebuilding homes destroyed by future disasters. The article says the measure would not apply to homes destroyed by last year’s fires, so treat it as policy background rather than an immediate rebuild action.

PCH safety remains a regional decision thread

The PCH documentary story matters beyond the premiere because Shane is pushing for systemic changes, including speed cameras and infrastructure fixes. For Palisades residents, the issue is less entertainment and more the safety of a route many people still depend on.

Fire/Rebuild Watch

Santa Ynez Reservoir and firefighting water remain watch-list issues

The reservoir story is the day’s clearest infrastructure concern. The open questions are operational: how long the reservoir remains out of service, what backup water arrangements are actually in place, and whether local fire stations are kept informed.

Unlicensed contractor charges remain a practical rebuild warning

MyNewsLA reports six allegedly unlicensed contractors were charged with illegally operating in the Palisades Fire area. This was covered recently, but the practical point remains current: verify contractor license status, down-payment rules and written scope before signing rebuild work.

Technical rebuild note: concrete foundations may need evidence-based review

A YA Group case study describes how testing distinguished damaged foundation components from concrete that could remain in service after the Palisades Fire. This is not broad policy news, but it is useful background if you are discussing slab, stem-wall or retaining-wall decisions with an engineer or insurer.
