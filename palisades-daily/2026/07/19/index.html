Temescal access is back, but not fully

The Palisadian-Post flyover marks Temescal Gateway Park’s reopening after the Palisades Fire. The Rivas and Sunset trails are accessible, while the Temescal Canyon Trail to the waterfall remains closed.

Why it matters: the park is usable again, but hikers should not assume the full pre-fire trail network is open.

Fire/Rebuild Watch

Modular-home permit pathway gets a practical explainer

An AB 818 rebuild explainer says the law, effective January 1, 2026, requires local agencies to approve or deny complete permit applications for state-approved modular homes within 10 business days during declared local emergencies.

Practical takeaway: if you are weighing a temporary or modular rebuild path, the important word is “complete.” The article says the clock starts only after the local agency has a complete application.

Commercial rebuild guide summarizes permitting steps

A Pacific Palisades fire rebuild guide lays out a contractor’s view of LADBS pathways, rebuilding centers, like-for-like +10% eligibility and claimed bottlenecks.

Practical takeaway: useful checklist material, but because it is a commercial construction source, verify any timeline, eligibility or cost implication with city staff and your own advisors.

Recovery advocates keep pressing for stronger coordination

A Center Square story republished by the Mountain Democrat quotes Palisades Recovery Coalition president Maryam Zar arguing that money, insurance disputes, business economics and lack of a dedicated recovery authority remain obstacles.

Practical takeaway: this is background unless you are tracking recovery governance or advocacy strategy; it does not create a new resident deadline.
