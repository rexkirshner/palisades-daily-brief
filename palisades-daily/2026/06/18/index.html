Executive Summary

Today’s most consequential Palisades item is the State Farm fight: survivors from the Palisades and Altadena fires are seeking formal standing in California’s enforcement proceeding. The practical question is whether survivors get a direct seat at the table in a process that could shape claims remedies and rebuild timing.

There is also an immediate administrative deadline: approved SBA disaster-loan recipients for Los Angeles wildfires must accept funds by June 30 through the MySBA Loan Portal.

Beyond fire recovery, the sources show small but meaningful community-return signals: local theater, retail, Palisades Village, parks planning, school support, and a school sun-safety effort.

Key Stories

Fire survivors seek formal role in State Farm case

Every Fire Survivor’s Network is asking to intervene in the California Department of Insurance enforcement proceeding over State Farm’s handling of Eaton and Palisades Fire claims. Circling The News reports the state has 15 days to decide on intervention.

Why it matters: if granted, the group could present survivor evidence, conduct discovery, cross-examine witnesses, and participate in settlement discussions. That could affect how strongly survivor experiences are represented in the official process.

Related reads: Palisadian-Post, Realtor.com, Consumer Watchdog.

SBA disaster-loan deadline is June 30

The U.S. Small Business Administration says Los Angeles wildfire survivors with approved disaster loans need to complete final steps to accept funds before the June 30 deadline.

Practical takeaway: if you were approved but have not accepted funds, check the MySBA Loan Portal.

Pepperdine legal clinic offers free services

The Palisadian-Post reports Pepperdine Caruso School of Law’s Disaster Relief Clinic is offering free legal services to Palisades residents.

This is potentially useful for residents navigating recovery paperwork, disputes, or legal questions, though the available article text here is brief.

Theatre Palisades returns to the stage

Theatre Palisades is returning for a one-night event at the Woman’s Club after the Palisades Fire destroyed the Pierson Playhouse.

Why it matters: it is a cultural recovery marker and a sign of local institutions finding temporary ways to operate.

Pali Trading Post plans July 1 opening

A Palisades Fire survivor is opening Pali Trading Post, a general store and gift shop in Pacific Palisades, on July 1, according to What Now Los Angeles.

The store is described as featuring products from Palisades-based brands and celebrating neighborhood history and identity.

Palisades Village reopening reported for Aug. 1

WWD reports Palisades Village will reopen Aug. 1 with returning retailers and new arrivals.

For residents, this is worth watching as a practical marker for the return of shopping, dining, and daily routines in the Village core.

Genesis gives to Palisades recovery and school efforts

Genesis contributed $10,000 across Tee It Up for Pali and the Palisades Elementary Charter School 5th Grade culmination, according to PR Newswire and a republished release in the Arizona Daily Star.

The local relevance is school recovery support and continued outside funding for Palisades-focused events.

Will Rogers and Topanga recovery planning

California State Parks says it has launched a recovery planning process for Will Rogers State Historic Park and Topanga State Park, with an emphasis on public participation.

Resident takeaway: this is the parks item to follow if you care about access, restoration priorities, and how public input will be handled.

SBA acceptance deadline

The SBA deadline is not a City decision, but it materially affects approved wildfire survivors and businesses. The action item is simple: confirm your portal status before June 30.
