Top 3

    LADWP told PPCC the Santa Ynez Reservoir cover replacement is expected to be complete and the reservoir refilled by December. Backup measures include bypass hoses, a Corbin-to-Temescal pipeline, emergency generators and brush clearance around facilities.

    A public safety report shared on LinkedIn says LAPD arrested a suspect after four alleged fires in the Palisades area. The practical takeaway is vigilance: citizen reporting and license-plate-reader alerts were described as part of the response.

Executive Summary

Today is not a single-big-decision day, but there are several useful local items.

The most practical civic update is LADWP’s water-system presentation: the Santa Ynez Reservoir cover replacement has a December target, pipe replacement is underway, and free water testing remains available. The biggest everyday-community item is the planned return of the July 4 celebration. Public safety also stays on the radar after a report of an arrest tied to alleged new fires.

For rebuilders, the recurring themes are still utilities, permits, insurance and tracking tools. Nothing in today’s material replaces reading the underlying items if you are making a rebuild or insurance decision.

Key Stories

  • Palisades Towne Hall remains one of the more concrete commercial-recovery efforts: a mobile, modular marketplace and gathering space planned for Via de la Paz. The project had raised more than $300,000 by June 21, according to the Palisadian-Post.
  • Local school hiring appeared in two listings: St. Matthew’s Parish School listed an early-childhood/kindergarten physical education and after-care role, and Palisades Charter High School listed a certificated school nurse position with a June 23 application deadline shown.
  • A Palisades Elementary science fair story is a community-life read rather than an urgent item; it highlights student participation and local school culture.

Decisions Affecting Palisades

  • PPCC’s leadership transition is underway, with Sue Kohl praised at the virtual meeting and Allison Holdorff Polhill stepping in as incoming president. Election notices and information are expected by July 1, and the next meeting is July 23.
  • The same PPCC/LADWP update says LADWP is replacing 100,000 linear feet of pipe across the Palisades over six years, with about 23,000 linear feet already replaced and 22 new hydrants installed along the route.
  • Circling The News reports that rebuild permit-fee forgiveness still had not been officially completed, with Councilmember Traci Park’s office saying the fees had moved through committees and would return to the full council this coming week before needing the mayor’s signature.

Fire/Rebuild Watch

  • The Pali Rebuild Map remains useful for parcel-level permit status, construction progress and estimated completion dates. The Palisades Rebuild Dashboard tracks median timing from plan submittal to permit issuance, permit to certificate of occupancy, and LADWP power-meter timing.
  • Circling The News continues to press the LADWP transite-pipe issue. If your property has older underground power infrastructure, the practical concern is potential private-contractor cost for temporary and permanent power connections.
  • NBC News reports a mistrial in the Jonathan Rinderknecht case, with a retrial set for Oct. 19 and Rinderknecht ordered to remain detained. For residents, this is background on fire-cause accountability, not an immediate action item.
  • The Real Deal included two Pacific Palisades projects in a citywide permit roundup: a wildfire rebuild on North Monument Street and an alteration project on North Las Lomas Avenue.

Around The Web

  • Realtor.com on Threads framed the Palisades as showing recovery signs, citing approved rebuilds, families moving into completed properties and an August reopening target for Palisades Village.
  • Fox Business covered the rebuild through a political lens, pairing construction progress with criticism of Mayor Karen Bass. Useful mainly as a signal of how national outlets are framing the Palisades recovery.
  • A LinkedIn real-estate post argues buyers and sellers need to treat insurance, permits, lot status and recent comparable sales as core diligence items after the fire.

Watch List

  • Watch for more complete July 4 logistics: parade access, closures, parking, safety plan and event schedule.
  • Watch the Santa Ynez Reservoir timeline through December, especially any changes to backup water-supply measures before refilling.
