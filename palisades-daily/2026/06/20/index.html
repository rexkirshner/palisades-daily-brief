Executive Summary

Today is not a quiet day for practical local items. The most resident-relevant updates are civic and operational: a June 22 city open house on the draft long-term recovery plan, a June 25 LADWP water-infrastructure meeting with questions due June 22, roadwork starting June 23, and public-safety concerns around vacant lots and Santa Monica Canyon.

Fire-recovery legal and insurance stories remain important, but there is no new resident action in the State Farm item beyond watching the California Department of Insurance decision window. More actionable recovery items today are the tax seminars, free Pepperdine legal clinic, and rebuild/insurability guidance.

Local everyday-life recovery continues: Sandy Days Kids Camp is back at Will Rogers State Beach, Palisades Village is targeting an August public reopening, and Pali Trading Post plans a July 1 opening on Sunset.

Key Stories

  • Crime reported in Palisades and Santa Monica Canyon: Circling The News reports an out-of-control party on vacant Marquez-area lots, recent thefts, burglaries, aggravated assaults, and Santa Monica Canyon incidents involving unhoused people. Practical takeaway: secure vacant or construction sites, use “no trespass” documentation where appropriate, and keep pressure on officials for response guidance.
  • CD11 Palisades update: Council District 11 says Sandy Days Kids Camp has returned to Will Rogers State Beach and that beautification crews are clearing trash bins, rights-of-way, and recovery debris. It also says Bienveneda emergency repairs remain on schedule, with weekday construction-hour traffic impacts.
  • Pali Trading Post plans July 1 opening: A new general store at 15248 W. Sunset Blvd. plans a July 1 ribbon cutting, with local brands and daily hours listed as 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. This is a tangible small-business recovery item, not just a rebuild process update.
  • Palisades Village reopening target: WWD reports tenants are targeting Aug. 1 readiness, with public reopening expected in mid-August. Useful mainly as a planning marker for local retail and foot traffic.

Decisions Affecting Palisades

  • Draft Long-Term Recovery Plan open house: The Mayor’s Office is holding a Palisades open house Monday, June 22, 6 to 8 p.m. at Palisades Lutheran Church. Residents can review long-term recovery and resilience projects, give feedback, and meet city department staff.
  • LADWP water-infrastructure meeting: PPCC’s Infrastructure Committee is hosting LADWP on Thursday, June 25, and is asking residents to submit water-infrastructure questions by June 22. If water pressure, rebuild connections, resilience, or service reliability matter to your property, this is one to track.
  • Road repair before July Fourth: The Pacific Palisades Community Council says work is scheduled to begin June 23 and be completed before the holiday weekend. Via de la Paz and other damaged streets are being assessed for further work; residents and contractors should expect possible traffic impacts and closures.
  • Measure ULA reform vote: LAist reports the L.A. City Council voted to develop a possible November ballot measure to reform the city’s real-estate transfer tax. This is citywide, but relevant to Palisades owners and developers because the tax applies to high-value real estate and some commercial property.

Fire/Rebuild Watch

  • Tax-solutions seminar for fire victims: A Zoom seminar on tax considerations for Pacific Palisades fire victims is scheduled for Tuesday, June 23, 9 to 10:30 a.m. It will include discussion of IRC Section 1033 involuntary conversion, which can matter when insurance proceeds are reinvested after a destroyed property.
  • PRC recovery talk on casualty-loss tax relief: CD11 also notes a June 23 Palisades Recovery Coalition talk focused on casualty-loss tax relief for disaster survivors. This overlaps with the tax theme and is worth checking if you are still sorting claim, deduction, or reinvestment questions.
  • Pepperdine disaster legal clinic: Pepperdine’s Disaster Relief Clinic is offering free legal help to Palisades and Eaton Fire survivors for insurance, remediation, FEMA claims, tenant issues, and related recovery questions. Residents can start with the online form, call 310-506-7449, or email the clinic.
  • Improving future insurability for standing homes: The Palisadian-Post looks at resilience upgrades for smoke- or fire-damaged standing homes, including roofs, vents, eaves, soffits, windows, exterior assemblies, and defensible-space decisions. Practical takeaway: if walls, roofs, or exteriors are already open, ask whether resilience work can be bundled into repairs.
  • Rebuild dashboard: Pali Builds is tracking median days from plan submittal to permit issuance, permit issuance to certificate of occupancy, and LADWP power-meter installation timing. Useful as a reality check against anecdotal rebuild timelines.
  • State Farm enforcement-case intervention request: Palisades and Altadena fire survivors are seeking formal standing in California’s State Farm enforcement case. Important background for policyholders, but not today’s lead because the immediate next step is awaiting the Department of Insurance decision window.
  • Why permit approval may not mean ready to build: Thomas James Homes argues that delays often happen before and after permit approval, including design selections, consultant coordination, and utility connections. Treat as a builder perspective, but the practical point is sound: plan utility and final-scope decisions early.

Around The Web

  • Malibu rebuild permits: Santa Monica Mirror reports Malibu has issued additional rebuild approvals and permits, with visible progress on Carbon Beach and a planned digital-permitting portal. This is outside Pacific Palisades, but relevant as a nearby comparison point for coastal rebuild logistics.
  • Teen swimmer’s school sun-safety push: A teen with Palisades school and swim ties founded Have Fun In The Sun, a nonprofit that has reached thousands of children with sun-safety education and donated gear. Local relevance is community/schools rather than fire recovery.
  • Theatre Palisades return: Theatre Palisades staged a community celebration at the Woman’s Club after losing the Pierson Playhouse in the fire. It is another sign of cultural life returning even before a permanent home is restored.
  • City Council honors PPCC President Sue Kohl: The L.A. City Council honored Sue Kohl for leading the Pacific Palisades Community Council through postfire recovery. This is civic recognition rather than a new resident action item.
