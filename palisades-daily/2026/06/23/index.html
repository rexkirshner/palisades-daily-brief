Executive Summary

Today is a practical-rebuild day rather than a major policy day.

The most useful new item is local: Ace Building Materials has opened on Sunset, adding nearby access to construction supplies. Road repairs are also scheduled to start today before July Fourth, which could affect access around work areas.

Fire recovery remains active in the background: residents are tracking water-infrastructure answers, rebuild timelines, insurance disputes, standing-home resilience upgrades, and soil-safety guidance. None of today’s legal or insurance items appears to create a new immediate resident deadline in the available reporting.

Key Stories

  • Ace Building Materials opens on the former Shell site. The new facility at 15401 West Sunset Boulevard is meant to give rebuilders a nearby source for construction and building supplies. City officials used temporary long-term zoning exceptions to allow the business to operate on the former gas station lot.
  • Palisades Towne Hall continues to gain support. Brian Garrett’s temporary marketplace and gathering-space concept at 859 and 861 Via de la Paz has surpassed its original crowdfunding goal and is designed to be modular and movable while permanent commercial rebuilding proceeds.
  • Crime and vacant-lot safety concerns remain unresolved. Circling The News reports resident complaints about parties on burned-out lots, burglaries involving construction equipment, and Santa Monica Canyon incidents. The practical takeaway: owners of vacant or active construction sites should tighten access, document property, and consider trespass paperwork where appropriate.

Decisions Affecting Palisades

  • Road repairs are scheduled ahead of July Fourth. The Pacific Palisades Community Council says work is scheduled to begin June 23 and finish before the holiday weekend, with additional high-priority areas still being assessed.
  • Will Rogers and Topanga recovery planning remains open for public involvement. California State Parks says there will be online and in-person opportunities and surveys for the recovery planning process. This matters for residents who use Will Rogers, Topanga, and nearby trail systems.
  • LADWP’s June 25 water-infrastructure discussion remains a key watch item. The meeting is expected to address Palisades water infrastructure questions raised after the fire, including reservoir and communication concerns.

Fire/Rebuild Watch

  • Standing-home owners may want to time resilience upgrades with repair work. The Palisadian-Post piece is not a promise about future insurance availability, but it flags practical areas to discuss with architects, builders, and insurers while walls, roofs, vents, landscaping, HVAC, or exterior systems are already being repaired.
  • The Palisades rebuild dashboard remains a useful progress tracker. Pali Builds tracks plan-submittal-to-permit timing, permit-to-certificate-of-occupancy timing, and LADWP power-meter installation timing for newly constructed homes.
  • LA County’s rebuilding page collects official tools. The page links to preapproved plans, manufactured homes, licensed contractor resources, self-certification, permit applications, fee information, debris removal, and temporary housing resources.
  • A builder’s rebuild-timeline guide emphasizes pre- and post-permit delays. The practical point is that permit approval alone does not mean a project is ready to build; design decisions, consultant coordination, scope, and utility connections can still drive schedule.
  • Wildfire survivors continue seeking a role in the State Farm proceeding. This remains important background for fire survivors, but today’s available reporting does not add a new resident action deadline.
  • A Palisades-linked soil-safety guide effort is underway. LightBox says it helped fund Purdue research to create a plain-language guide for post-fire soil testing, cleanup, and restoration decisions.

Around The Web

  • The New York Post covered families moving back and Palisades Village reopening. Treat this as broader attention on the recovery mood, not a substitute for local service-by-service reopening details.
  • A New York Post opinion piece framed construction as the new sound of the Palisades. Useful mainly as outside commentary on the rebuild atmosphere.

Watch List

  • Palisades News homepage is flagging a Santa Monica Airport “Great Park” item and a City Council item about a possible Measure ULA exemption for Palisades fire victims. The available page text is enough to watch both topics, but not enough here to summarize the procedural details confidently.
