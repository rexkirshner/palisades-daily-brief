Executive Summary

Today is fairly quiet, but there are several practical local items worth scanning.

The most time-sensitive item is the possible June 30 SBA disaster-loan disbursement deadline for approved borrowers. Beyond that, the useful updates are community-facing: July 4 planning, road work, Will Rogers recovery participation, and a new resident memory archive.

Fire-rebuild process stories continue, but most are background unless you are actively rebuilding, dealing with utilities, tracking permits, or following the arson case.

Key Stories

  • SBA disaster-loan deadline clarification: Approved borrowers after the January 2025 fires may have a June 30 deadline to disburse and draw down funds. The city update says borrowers should contact their SBA case manager, use the MySBA portal, or visit the Pali Hub for help.
  • Fourth of July celebration returns next weekend: CD11 says the 77th annual Pacific Palisades July 4th Parade will run through the Historic Village, followed by an evening celebration at Palisades High School.
  • Asphalt repairs have begun throughout the Palisades: Street Services work is expected to continue over coming weeks. Expect crews, large trucks, construction equipment, and neighborhood traffic friction.
  • “What Remains” archive opens for Palisades stories: Blythe Gering’s project collects photos and stories of objects residents saved or found after the fire. Residents who want to contribute can do so through the archive site named in the article.
  • Will Rogers State Historic Park recovery and events: State Parks points residents to recovery-planning participation for Topanga and Will Rogers, and lists upcoming July events at Will Rogers, including guided history and nature programs.

Decisions Affecting Palisades

  • Potrero Canyon pedestrian/bicycle bridge process is moving forward: The city is asking residents, business owners, and stakeholders to reengage and provide input on the bridge connecting George Wolfberg Park and Will Rogers State Beach across PCH.
  • Bienveneda Avenue emergency repairs remain on schedule: Construction hours are Monday through Friday, 7 a.m. to 4 p.m., with traffic impacts expected. The city lists Superintendent Chris Ochoa at 310-505-7395 for questions.
  • Permit-fee waivers are still not final: Circling The News reports that no fees have been officially forgiven yet, with the issue expected to return to the full City Council and then require the mayor’s signature.
  • Federal recovery money remains a watch item: The same report says federal funding through FEMA and disaster recovery grants was discussed, but no money was confirmed for items such as sidewalks and roads.

Fire/Rebuild Watch

  • Pali Rebuild Map remains a practical tool for checking parcel-level permit status, construction progress, and estimated completion dates. Useful if you are tracking your block or comparing rebuild momentum.
  • Pacific Palisades Rebuild Dashboard tracks median timing from plan submittal to permit issuance, permit to certificate of occupancy, and LADWP power-meter timing.
  • Pali Builds positions itself as a live rebuild resource with permit data, contractor directory, and insurance tools.
  • LADWP transite-pipe policy remains a high-cost issue for many rebuilding homeowners with older underground power connections. The practical point: if your lot has transite pipe, utility hookup strategy could affect timing and cost.
  • Rinderknecht arson case: NBC News reports a mistrial after jurors deadlocked, with retrial set to begin Oct. 19. This is important background, but not an immediate resident action item.
  • Local fire-response accountability commentary and Station 23/69 commentary continue the debate over responsibility for the Palisades Fire response.

Around The Web

  • 121 Design Build frames the rebuild as moving from emergency recovery into visible construction, with permit strategy, inspections, utilities, insurance, and sequencing as the key homeowner issues.
  • AOL and Fox Business carried outside commentary about the rebuild and criticism of Mayor Karen Bass. Useful mainly as a read on national attention, not as a local how-to.
  • A backyard-remodeling firm has a Palisades-focused page discussing canyon lots, defensible space, drainage, and hillside design. Treat it as vendor guidance, not civic news.

Watch List

  • July 4 logistics: CD11 says more event details are coming for the parade and Palisades High evening celebration.
  • Potrero Canyon bridge input: watch for meeting dates, survey links, or design materials from the Bureau of Engineering.
  • Permit-fee waiver: watch whether the full City Council and mayor finalize any fee forgiveness, and whether refunds become available for residents who already paid.
  • Utility-hookup costs: if you are rebuilding on an older underground-power lot, keep watching the LADWP transite-pipe issue.
  • Will Rogers and Topanga recovery planning: State Parks is directing residents to the recovery-planning participation site for future updates and engagement opportunities.
