Executive Summary

Today’s brief is practical and deadline-driven. The most immediate action item is submitting questions for LADWP’s June 25 water-infrastructure meeting by 11 p.m. tonight.

The most locally significant new community item is Palisades Towne Hall, a resident-led effort to create a temporary marketplace and gathering place on Via de la Paz. It is not a substitute for permanent commercial rebuilding, but it could help restore everyday community life while that process plays out.

Fire-recovery items remain active but should mostly be treated as watch-and-act items: brush clearance, road repairs, tax sessions, permitting resources, and tonight’s Long-Term Recovery Plan open house.

Key Stories

  • Palisades Towne Hall would be built on short-term leased parcels at 859 and 861 Via de la Paz. The plan is deliberately movable, so the site can shift when landlords are ready to rebuild permanent commercial buildings.
  • Crime and vacant-lot safety concerns remain on the radar. Circling the News reported recent burglaries, assaults, construction-equipment thefts, and complaints about parties on burned-out lots; residents with active sites should keep access, lighting, cameras, and “no trespass” paperwork in mind.
  • Sandy Days Kids Camp has returned to Will Rogers State Beach, according to CD11’s update. That is a small but concrete sign of normal summer routines returning for local families.

Decisions Affecting Palisades

  • LADWP water meeting: questions due today. PPCC will host LADWP at 6 p.m. June 25; residents are being asked to submit water-infrastructure questions by 11 p.m. June 22.
  • The City’s Draft Long-Term Recovery Plan open house is tonight, June 22 from 6 to 8 p.m. at Palisades Lutheran Church. This is the main civic-feedback opportunity in today’s sources for long-term recovery and resilience projects.
  • City Council advanced a possible Measure ULA exemption for certain Palisades Fire victims. The proposal would still require voter approval in November 2026 and would create a one-time exemption for qualifying residential property owners whose homes were damaged or destroyed.
  • Bienveneda Avenue emergency repairs are continuing and remain on schedule, according to CD11. Expect construction-hour traffic impacts Monday through Friday, 7 a.m. to 4 p.m.
  • Road repairs before July Fourth are scheduled to begin June 23, with possible temporary traffic impacts and road closures. PPCC is also seeking additional repairs on Via de la Paz and other damaged streets.

Fire/Rebuild Watch

  • Brush clearance: Owners of vacant or fire-damaged lots should inspect regrowth now. CD11 says unmanaged brush can be a fire risk, especially near homes under construction.
  • Tax-relief seminar is scheduled for Tuesday, June 23 from 9 to 10:30 a.m. on Zoom. It focuses on tax considerations for Palisades Fire victims, including involuntary conversion issues.
  • Palisades Recovery Coalition’s June 23 Recovery Talk also focuses on casualty-loss tax relief for disaster survivors.
  • Pali Builds rebuild dashboard continues tracking median days from plan submittal to permit issuance, permit issuance to certificate of occupancy, and LADWP power-meter installation timing.
  • LA County’s rebuilding page remains the best general portal in today’s sources for rebuilding tools, including preapproved plans, permit applications, fee deferrals, debris removal, and temporary housing links.
  • Future insurability for standing homes is a useful background read for owners already doing smoke, HVAC, roof, siding, vent, window, landscape, or exterior repairs. The practical point: combine required repair work with resilience upgrades where it makes economic sense.

Around The Web

  • Palisades Village reopening coverage continues to circulate nationally, with reopening framed as a major symbol of neighborhood recovery. The coverage also repeats rebuild-permit figures attributed to Pali Builds.
  • Pali Trading Post is still worth noting for July 1: the Sunset Boulevard shop plans a ribbon cutting, local brands, food, refreshments, and music.
  • A Pacific Palisades hate-crime case is drawing outside coverage. The Pride LA reports felony charges with hate-crime enhancements tied to alleged threats against Rabbi Zushe Cunin of Chabad of Pacific Palisades.

Watch List

  • Whether LADWP answers resident questions directly on the Santa Ynez Reservoir, fire-station notification, and coordination with LAFD at the June 25 PPCC meeting.
  • Whether Palisades Towne Hall clears the practical hurdles that matter to residents: permits, opening date, vendor mix, parking, hours, and whether it becomes a real daily-use hub.
  • Whether LAFD brush-clearance notices become a widespread compliance issue for vacant lots, especially near active rebuilds.
  • Whether the Measure ULA exemption advances from City Attorney drafting to actual ballot language and who would qualify.
  • Whether road repairs starting June 23 create meaningful access issues on key local routes before July Fourth events.
