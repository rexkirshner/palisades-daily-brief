Executive Summary

Today is relatively quiet, but there are several practical items worth tracking.

The most resident-useful news is operational: a new local shop is preparing to open, road work starts June 23, and fire survivors have near-term tax-relief sessions. City recovery planning remains active, with the June 22 Long-Term Recovery Plan open house and a June 25 LADWP water infrastructure meeting.

Insurance, permitting, and rebuild-process stories continue in the background. They matter, but today’s new actionable items are mostly dates, access, and community-return markers rather than major policy changes.

Key Stories

New local retail: Pali Trading Post

Pali Trading Post is set to open July 1 at 15248 W. Sunset Blvd. The store is described as a modern general store and gift shop featuring Palisades-founded brands, with a ribbon-cutting scheduled for 11:25 a.m.

What Now Los Angeles also reports the store is owned by Michelle Murphy, a Palisades fire survivor. For residents, this is a concrete local-business reopening milestone rather than just another recovery-process update.

Road work before July Fourth

Palisades road repair work is scheduled to begin June 23 and be completed before the July Fourth holiday weekend. PPCC has also identified other high-priority areas, including Via de la Paz, for assessment and future repairs.

Practical takeaway: plan for temporary traffic impacts or closures, especially if you are coordinating construction access, errands, or holiday-weekend plans.

Crime and vacant-lot safety concerns

Circling The News reports concerns about a large party on vacant lots in the Marquez area, construction-site thefts, burglaries, aggravated assaults, and incidents in Santa Monica Canyon.

The practical issue is security around vacant lots, construction sites, and standing homes. If you have a property under repair or rebuild, the story is worth reading for the types of incidents being reported.

Decisions Affecting Palisades

Long-Term Recovery Plan open house is June 22

The Mayor’s Office is hosting a Draft Long-Term Recovery Plan open house on Monday, June 22, from 6 to 8 p.m. at Palisades Lutheran Church.

This is the main near-term venue in today’s sources for residents to give input on long-term recovery and resilience projects.

LADWP water infrastructure meeting is June 25

The PPCC Infrastructure Committee is hosting LADWP on Thursday, June 25, for a meeting focused on Pacific Palisades water infrastructure. The committee is asking residents to submit water infrastructure questions by June 22.

If your rebuild, hillside property, or neighborhood access depends on water-system reliability, this is worth tracking.

Bienveneda emergency repairs continue

The Bienveneda Avenue emergency repairs project remains underway, with construction impacts expected Monday through Friday from 7 a.m. to 4 p.m. The city lists Superintendent Chris Ochoa at 310-505-7395 for project questions.

Fire/Rebuild Watch

Tax-relief sessions on June 23

A Zoom seminar from 9 to 10:30 a.m. June 23 will cover tax considerations for Pacific Palisades fire victims, including involuntary conversion issues.

Separately, the Palisades Recovery Coalition’s June 23 Recovery Talk focuses on casualty-loss tax relief for survivors of wildfires, hurricanes, and floods.

State Farm dispute remains a major background issue

The Palisadian-Post reports wildfire survivors from the Pacific Palisades and Altadena fires have asked California regulators for legal standing in the state enforcement case against State Farm. The group expects a decision from the California Department of Insurance within 15 days of its petition.

Circling The News also covered the press conference and survivor complaints. This remains important for affected policyholders, but today’s sources do not add a new resident deadline beyond the pending regulator decision.

Standing-home owners: use repairs to think about future insurability

The Palisadian-Post highlights resilience upgrades for standing homes undergoing smoke remediation or repairs, including roof condition, ember-resistant vents, eaves, soffits, exterior materials, windows, and landscaping.

Practical takeaway: if walls, roofs, HVAC, or exterior systems are already being opened up, it may be cheaper to evaluate resilience upgrades now than after repairs are complete.

Rebuild tracking and process resources

The Palisades rebuild dashboard tracks median days from plan submittal to permit issuance, time from permit issuance to certificate of occupancy, and LADWP power-meter installation timing.

LA County’s rebuilding page collects permit, express-lane, preapproved-plan, contractor, fee, and temporary-housing resources. A Thomas James Homes rebuild timeline piece emphasizes that permit approval is not the same as being ready to build, because design selections, scope, utilities, and agency approvals can still drive delays.

Around The Web

Palisades Village reopening target

WWD reports that Palisades Village tenants are targeting Aug. 1 for spaces to be finalized and ready, with public reopening slated for mid-August.

For residents, this is a meaningful everyday-life marker, especially for retail access and the symbolic return of the Village center.

Sandy Days Kids Camp returns to Will Rogers State Beach

Council District 11 reports Sandy Days Kids Camp has returned to its longtime home at Will Rogers State Beach after temporarily relocating following last year’s fires.

Teen sun-safety nonprofit with Palisades roots

The Palisadian-Post profiles Pasha Selig, a former Village School student and swimmer who founded Have Fun In The Sun, a nonprofit teaching elementary students about sun safety.
